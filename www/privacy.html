Privacy Policy

Mail Client for VS Code

Last updated: 8 July 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how the Mail Client extension for Visual Studio Code (the “Extension”), published by Jan Brchan (the “Developer”, “we”, “us”), handles your information.

1. Summary

The Extension is a client-side application that runs entirely on your own device inside Visual Studio Code. We do not operate any server, we do not collect, store, or transmit your personal data or email content to ourselves or to any third party, and we do not use it for advertising or analytics.

2. Information the Extension accesses

To provide email functionality, the Extension accesses, on your device only:

  • Your mailbox contents (messages, folders, attachments, and related metadata) retrieved from your email provider over IMAP, and messages you send over SMTP.
  • Account configuration you enter (email address, server host and port, display name).
  • Authentication credentials — either a password or, for OAuth2 accounts, an OAuth refresh/access token.

3. Google user data

If you connect a Gmail account, the Extension requests the Google OAuth scope https://mail.google.com/, which permits access to your Gmail mailbox over IMAP and SMTP. This access is used solely to let you read, search, compose, and send email from within your editor.

  • Google user data is processed locally, in memory, on your device to display and send mail.
  • Google user data is never transmitted to the Developer or to any third-party server.
  • Google user data is not sold, and is not used for advertising or any purpose other than providing the email features you request.
  • The only credential retained is the OAuth refresh token, stored locally (see Section 4). No email content is persisted by the Extension beyond your current session.

The Extension's use and transfer of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

4. Where data is stored

  • Credentials and OAuth tokens are stored using the VS Code Secret Storage, which uses your operating system's secure credential store (Keychain, Windows Credential Manager, or libsecret).
  • Non-sensitive settings (account list, server addresses, preferences) are stored in VS Code's local extension storage on your device.
  • Email content is held only transiently in memory while you view or send it, and is not written to any database or sent to us.

5. Data sharing

We do not share your data with anyone, because we never receive it. Email data flows directly between your device and your email provider (e.g. Google, Microsoft, or your own IMAP/SMTP server) over encrypted connections (TLS).

6. Data retention and deletion

Because data is stored only on your device, you are in full control. Removing an account in the Extension deletes its stored credentials and OAuth token. Uninstalling the Extension removes its local data. You can also revoke the Extension's access to your Google account at any time at Google Account › Third-party access.

7. Security

Connections to mail servers use TLS. Credentials are held in the operating system's secure store. OAuth sign-in uses the industry-standard Authorization Code flow with PKCE. However, no method of transmission or storage is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

8. Children

The Extension is not directed to children under the age of 13.

9. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated “Last updated” date.
